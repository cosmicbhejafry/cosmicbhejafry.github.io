z How I prepared for the GRE & TOEFL

Note: This way of preparation was the most efficient for me, it might not be for you. Please take everything I say with a pinch of salt; keep in mind that the schedule I outline here is highly dependent on my interests and skills.

Some context: I gave the TOEFL a few days before the GRE. Both given in August 2021.

Also- TL;DR: gregmat is cool, I used stuff from his free materials list.

TOEFL

I started ~3-4 days before the exam. I prepared as follows:

  • Reading & Listening:

    • Skimmed through the video transcripts from this official EdX course and gave the quizzes there. This link is equivalent to the EdX course in case you’re too lazy to signup/login etc.

  • Speaking:

    • This was unfamiliar, prepping for this took up most of my time.
    • Used GREG MAT’s advice on having a predetermined structure for the speaking part: link to youtube
    • note that GREG MAT’s advice is for the old TOEFL speaking structure which had 6 questions, if you plan on using it just make sure you only watch the bits useful for the new 4 question speaking test.
    • The way I practised: timer and record myself speaking.
    • Practise questions from:
      • official EdX course
      • TOEFL Speaking practice test
    • very useful: used this youtube video to simulate taking the final test after practising a little.

  • Writing:

    • Structure I followed for:
      • Integrated writing task: similar to GRE’s Issue essay
      • Independent writing task: referred to this
    • Prep: wrote one essay for each task before the exam. From here .

[I got 119/120]

GRE

  • Prep: the 2 free mocks provided by ETS.

    • Before starting any prep gave the mock test to see how much and what I need to prepare, didn’t give writing section in the first attempt.
    • Gave the writing section (timed) of the first mock after familiarizing myself with a structure
    • The day before the exam: gave the other free mock test, this time with writing.

  • Writing:

    • The first 2 videos on this link

  • Verbal:

    • Used the Magoosh app for vocab, started seriously ~1 week before the exam.
    • I struggled the most with the blanks in this section
    • Practised from a recommended book [please contact me via email or social media if you’d like to know more for legal rzns]

  • Quantitative:

    • Just saw those statistic formulas and some geometry results the night before the exam.

[I got 5W, 160V, 170Q]
